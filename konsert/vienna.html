The first time I visited Vienna, I was 25, young and foolish..



It feels like a lifetime ago.



I was on a monthlong hitchhiking trip through Europe, and stayed a night in Vienna while making my way home to Copenhagen, where I was studying my jazz piano masters degree.





After 17 years of frenetic jazz piano practice, I felt the need to revolt against all the prestigiousness I experienced in the jazz world.



Hence, I had brought my guitar and traveled from city to city playing and singing as BADLY and embarrassingly as I could.





Here's a summary of how it went:



- My first stop was Berlin, where I apparently fit in way too well‚Ä¶ not many people reacted.



- In Krakow I wasn't allowed to play anywhere.



- The further east I went, the more of a "success" it became. I remember playing for a FULL square of jaws dropped in Lviv, where nobody could grasp how I could do something so idiotic. My guitar case was filled over the edge with money (which sadly wasn't worth much).



- In Romania people just got upset.



Once I got to Vienna, I remember feeling like the soul behind all its majestic and proud buildings would come haunt me if I stained the city with my performance‚Ä¶



I also didn't have time, cause I met too many nice people!!
